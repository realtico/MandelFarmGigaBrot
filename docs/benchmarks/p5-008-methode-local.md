## Initial Interpretation

The fixed row-band scheduler is useful as a baseline, but it mixes two effects:
parallelism and load balance. More threads can help because the image is split
into more bands, not only because the CPU has more parallel capacity.

The dynamic tile scheduler makes the plateau clearer. On this machine, the hard
scene appears to reach its useful range around 10 to 12 worker threads. Past
that point, extra threads mostly add scheduling and synchronization overhead.

Tile size matters because it controls the number of jobs in the queue:

```text
small tiles: more jobs, better load balance, more queue traffic
large tiles: fewer jobs, less queue traffic, more idle-worker risk
```

For the hard scene at 1024x768 and max_iter 2000, early manual runs suggested
that tiles around 32 or 64 pixels are the most interesting candidates on
Methode. The generated JSON files in this directory are the reproducible record
for checking that conclusion.

Current reading:

- `tile_size=16` and `tile_size=32` are the best candidates in this capture.
- `tile_size=64` is close, but already a little slower at 10 threads.
- `tile_size=512` creates only 4 jobs, so it cannot feed 10 worker threads.
- The dynamic scheduler makes load balance much clearer than fixed bands.
- The useful thread range for this scene on Methode is roughly 10 to 16, with
  variation between captures. More than that does not show a reliable gain.
